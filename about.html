About “Returning to Haifa”

← Back to Home

Returning to Haifa carries the name of the novel written by Ghassan Kanafani, one of the most powerful Palestinian voices ever silenced by the occupation. This title is not literary decoration — it is inheritance, memory, and resistance.

In Returning to Haifa, Kanafani confronts the brutal meaning of dispossession and the impossible grief of a family returning to a home stolen through force. By the end of the novel, the truth stands naked: liberation cannot come from empty talks, negotiations, or illusions. It requires resistance — real resistance — against a settler project built on violence and erasure.

Ghassan Kanafani knew this truth deeply, and he paid for it with his life. In 1972, the Zionist occupation assassinated him in Beirut through a car bomb. They murdered him alongside his 17-year-old niece Lamis, a young Palestinian girl he loved fiercely and protected with all the tenderness he had.

Their killing was not random. It was a targeted attempt to silence a revolutionary mind, a writer whose words sharpened the consciousness of an entire people, and a man who refused to normalize the violence of occupation. They murdered Lamis because they wanted to crush his heart — and through him, crush the spirit of Palestinian resistance.

This page, and this website, exist to keep their memory alive. Ghassan's clarity, his refusal, his belief that literature and resistance walk side by side — and Lamis, whose life was stolen before it could fully bloom — are the heartbeat behind this project.

Every word published here refuses erasure. Every page rejects colonial language. Every sentence honors the legacy of a man who taught us that truth without courage is worthless.
